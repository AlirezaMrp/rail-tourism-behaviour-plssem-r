# A PLS-SEM Model to Analyse Rail Tourists' Perceptions in Relation to Current Tourism Trends

Behavioural modelling research project developed within the UIC TopRail “Future Rail Tourism” initiative, focusing on how emerging tourism trends influence rail tourists’ perceptions, preferences and behavioural intentions.

This project applies Partial Least Squares Structural Equation Modelling (PLS-SEM) using SmartPLS to analyse the relationships between sustainability perceptions, digital technologies, post-pandemic behavioural changes, decentralised tourism preferences and experience-oriented travel in the context of rail tourism.

The study investigates how these contemporary tourism trends shape Rail Tourism Preference (RTP) and contribute to strategic planning insights for future passenger rail systems and sustainable tourism development.

---

## Research Objectives

- Analyse the behavioural drivers influencing rail tourism preferences
- Examine the role of sustainability and digitalisation in rail travel behaviour
- Evaluate post-pandemic changes in tourism and mobility perceptions
- Investigate how decentralised and rural-access tourism trends affect rail travel demand
- Support future strategic planning for sustainable and experience-oriented rail tourism

---

## Methodology

The project applies a quantitative behavioural modelling framework using:

- Partial Least Squares Structural Equation Modelling (PLS-SEM)
- Reflective measurement modelling
- Structural path analysis
- Reliability and validity assessment
- Bootstrapping procedures
- Multi-Group Analysis (MGA)

The analytical workflow was conducted in SmartPLS.
